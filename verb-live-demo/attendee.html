Verb Attendee demo

Demo details

This demo shows how an attendee can participate in a speaker's broadcast, all while trying to keep resources usage low. Specifically, the attendee will receive the broadcast via a Streaming plugin mountpoint (that the speaker forwards their media to). Using a Streaming plugin mountpoint makes it easy to scale the broadcast, as the same mountpoint could be configured on different Janus instances.

Whenever the participant wants to make a question, they can click the hand icon (), which will do two things:

  1. connect to the AudioBridge as an active participant;
  2. mute the audio of the incoming Streaming mountpoint.

Connecting to the AudioBridge will ensure they'll be able to talk to the speaker, and since the AudioBridge mix is forwarded to the Streaming mountpoint itself, all other attendees will hear the question too (which is why the attendee making the question should mute the Streaming mountpoint, or they'll hear their own voice back). Notice that, for the sake of simplicity, we're assuming that the AudioBridge hosting the speaker and the Streaming mountpoint serving the attendee are on the same server: in a more scalable scenario (where multiple Streaming mountpoints may be distributing the same broadcast) this assumption may be incorrect.

Notice that, in this simple demo, no moderation is used, so clicking the hand icon immediately grants the attendee access to the room, and speaking rights in the broadcast. In order to avoid disruptions or abuses, it's recommended to add a moderation aspect in production.

Press the Start button above to launch the demo.
